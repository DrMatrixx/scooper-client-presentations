The numbers tell a clear story about what manual operations cost a specialist e-commerce business like Wifimedia:

**Missed Conversions:** The average e-commerce conversion rate sits between 2–4% globally. For consumer electronics specifically, it tends toward the lower end due to longer research cycles (customers spend an estimated 79 days researching before purchasing electronics). Every percentage point improvement on Wifimedia's traffic directly translates to recovered revenue.

**Slow Lead Response:** Industry data suggests the average business takes 47 hours to respond to a lead. Yet responding within 5 minutes increases conversion likelihood by up to 100x compared to a 30-minute delay. For a specialist retailer where pre-sales advice is the differentiator, every hour of delay pushes the customer toward Amazon or Coolblue — competitors who can't match your expertise but can match your response speed.

**Cart Abandonment:** Approximately 70% of online shopping carts are abandoned before purchase. For a retailer selling €500+ soundbars, headphones, and networking equipment, each abandoned cart represents significant lost revenue. Automated recovery sequences typically recapture 5–15% of abandoned carts.

**Experience Center Underutilization:** Your Arnhem Experience Center is a massive competitive advantage — customers rave about it in reviews. But without automated booking, follow-up, and post-visit nurture sequences, the gap between "amazing in-store experience" and "completed online purchase" is likely wider than it needs to be.

**Conservative annual estimate of recoverable value: €75,000–€150,000** — through faster response times, reduced cart abandonment, improved conversion rates, and freed team capacity redirected toward high-value activities like B2B Projects and Experience Center engagement.
